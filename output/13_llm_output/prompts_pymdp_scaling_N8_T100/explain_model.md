# EXPLAIN_MODEL

This repository contains a comprehensive overview of the Active Inference (A-Inf) framework used to generate AML models from probabilistic graphical models (PGMs). The A-Inf framework is designed to provide insights into how data can be transformed into new objects and actions, which are then represented in the model.

The repository includes:

1. **Overview**: A comprehensive overview of the A-Inf framework used for generating AML models from probabilistic graphical models (PGMs). This document provides a concise description of the core components involved in A-Inf, including how data is transformed into new objects and actions, and key relationships between them.
2. **Model Purpose**: A detailed explanation of what A-Inf does, its purpose, and what it represents. The repository includes information on:
   - What are the hidden states (s_f0, s_f1, etc.) and what they represent?
   - What are the observations (o_m0, o_m1, etc.) and what do they capture?
   - What actions/controls (u_c0, π_c0, etc.) are available and what do they do?

3. **Core Components**: The repository includes information on:
   - What are the hidden states (s_f0, s_f1, etc.) and what they represent?
   - What are the observations (o_m0, o_m1, etc.) and what do they capture?
   - What actions/controls (u_c0, π_c0, etc.) are available and what do they do?

4. **Model Dynamics**: The repository includes information on:
   - How does A-Inf implement Active Inference principles?
   - Key relationships between the model's beliefs and their predictions.
   - What can be learned or predicted using A-Inf.
